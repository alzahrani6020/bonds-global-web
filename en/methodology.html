Calculator 1

Break-Even Calculator

All formulas used to calculate the break-even point and net profitability.

📐 Core Formulas

Contribution Margin = Selling Price − Variable Cost per Unit
Break-Even (units) = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin
Break-Even (amount) = Break-Even Units × Selling Price
Revenue = Quantity × Selling Price
Total Costs = Fixed Costs + (Variable Cost × Quantity)
Gross Profit = Revenue − Total Costs
Tax = max(0, Gross Profit × Tax Rate)
Profit After Tax = Gross Profit − Tax
Zakat = max(0, Profit After Tax × Zakat Rate)
Net Profit = Profit After Tax − Zakat
Profit Margin = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
ROI = (Net Profit ÷ Total Costs) × 100

🧮 Step-by-Step Numeric Example

Data: Fixed Costs = 10,000 | Selling Price = 500 | Variable Cost = 300 | Quantity = 100 | Tax = 15% | Zakat = 2.5%

Step 1: Contribution Margin

500 − 300 = 200

Step 2: Break-Even (units)

10,000 ÷ 200 = 50 units

Step 3: Break-Even (amount)

50 × 500 = 25,000

Step 4: Revenue

100 × 500 = 50,000

Step 5: Total Costs

10,000 + (300 × 100) = 10,000 + 30,000 = 40,000

Step 6: Gross Profit

50,000 − 40,000 = 10,000

Step 7: Tax

max(0, 10,000 × 0.15) = 1,500

Step 8: Profit After Tax

10,000 − 1,500 = 8,500

Step 9: Zakat

max(0, 8,500 × 0.025) = 212.5

Step 10: Final Net Profit

8,500 − 212.5 = 8,287.5

Step 11: Profit Margin

(8,287.5 ÷ 50,000) × 100 = 16.58%

Step 12: Return on Investment (ROI)

(8,287.5 ÷ 40,000) × 100 = 20.72%

Pricing Calculator

Formulas for determining the optimal selling price based on cost and target margin.

📐 Core Formulas

Cost per Unit = Direct Cost + Overhead per Unit
Selling Price = Cost per Unit ÷ (1 − Desired Margin%)
Profit Before Tax = Selling Price − Cost per Unit
Tax = max(0, Profit Before Tax × Tax Rate)
Zakat = max(0, (Profit Before Tax − Tax) × Zakat Rate)
Net Profit per Unit = Profit Before Tax − Tax − Zakat
Monthly Profit = Net Profit per Unit × Monthly Volume
Actual Margin = (Net Profit per Unit ÷ Selling Price) × 100
Break-Even Price = Cost per Unit + (Monthly Fixed Costs ÷ Monthly Volume)

🧮 Step-by-Step Numeric Example

Data: Direct Cost = 200 | Overhead per Unit = 50 | Target Margin = 30% | Monthly Volume = 100 | Monthly Fixed Costs = 5,000 | Tax = 15% | Zakat = 2.5%

Step 1: Cost per Unit

200 + 50 = 250

Step 2: Selling Price

250 ÷ (1 − 0.30) = 250 ÷ 0.70 = 357.14

Step 3: Profit Before Tax

357.14 − 250 = 107.14

Step 4: Tax

max(0, 107.14 × 0.15) = 16.07

Step 5: Zakat

max(0, (107.14 − 16.07) × 0.025) = 91.07 × 0.025 = 2.28

Step 6: Net Profit per Unit

107.14 − 16.07 − 2.28 = 88.79

Step 7: Monthly Profit

88.79 × 100 = 8,879

Step 8: Actual Margin

(88.79 ÷ 357.14) × 100 = 24.86%

Step 9: Break-Even Price

250 + (5,000 ÷ 100) = 250 + 50 = 300

Cash Flow Calculator

Formulas for tracking opening and closing balances of monthly cash flows.

📐 Core Formulas

Opening Balance (Month 1) = User Input
Opening Balance (Month N) = Closing Balance of Month N−1
Total Inflows = Sum of all inflow categories
Total Outflows = Sum of all outflow categories
Net Cash Flow = Total Inflows − Total Outflows
Closing Balance = Opening Balance + Net Cash Flow

📋 Logical Explanation

  • Opening Balance for Month 1 is entered directly by the user.
  • Opening Balance for any subsequent month equals the previous month's closing balance automatically.
  • Total Inflows is the sum of all income categories: sales, collections, loans, etc.
  • Total Outflows is the sum of all expense categories: salaries, rent, purchases, etc.
  • Net Cash Flow being positive means increased liquidity; negative means a deficit.
  • Closing Balance is calculated cumulatively to give a clear picture of the cash position.

Calculator 4

Loan & Financing Calculator

Loan formulas using fixed-rate amortization and equal installment payments.

📐 Core Formulas

Net Loan = Loan Amount − Down Payment + Extra Fees
Periodic Rate = Annual Rate ÷ Periods per Year
Installment = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]
Total Interest = (Installment × Number of Payments) − Net Loan
EAR = (1 + Periodic Rate)^Periods per Year − 1

Where: P = Net Loan | r = Periodic Interest Rate | n = Number of Payments

📋 Logical Explanation

  • Net Loan deducts the down payment and adds any administrative fees.
  • Periodic Rate converts the annual interest rate into the installment period (monthly, quarterly, etc.).
  • Installment Formula is the internationally recognized amortization formula.
  • Total Interest is calculated by multiplying the installment by the number of payments, then subtracting the net loan.
  • Effective Annual Rate (EAR) reflects the true cost considering the frequency of periods.

Important Warnings

Assumptions & Limitations

Users should understand that these calculators are estimation tools, not a substitute for professional advice.

⚠️ Calculator Assumptions

  • Tax is calculated on positive profit only — no tax loss carryforward.
  • Zakat is calculated on profit after tax according to the Saudi standard.
  • The loan calculator uses fixed-rate amortization (not variable rate).
  • Cash flow does not account for the time value of money.
  • All calculations use nominal values and are not inflation-adjusted.

📋 Usage Limitations

  • Results depend entirely on the accuracy of user-entered data.
  • Calculators do not include indirect bank commissions or additional financing fees.
  • Assumes fixed and variable costs remain constant during the studied period.
  • Does not provide binding investment or tax recommendations.
  • For complex financial decisions, consult a licensed accountant or financial advisor.
